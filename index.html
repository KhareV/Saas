Isolated systems

Agents are effective at narrow use cases, showing success in verticals like customer support and code generation. Humans must coordinate across functions, and spend large amounts of time reviewing outputs.

Fig. 1 Isolated system of specialised agents

Limited integration

Current AI tools operate in silos, unable to communicate or share context across different workflows. Data transfer between systems requires manual intervention and creates bottlenecks in automation.

Fig. 2 Disconnected workflow management

Manual oversight

Every automated task requires human verification and quality control before implementation. The need for constant monitoring limits scalability and creates dependency on human resources.

Fig. 3 Human-dependent quality control

Context loss

Information gets fragmented as it moves between different specialized agents and systems. Critical business context is lost in translation, leading to inconsistent outputs and errors.

Fig. 4 Fragmented information flow

Scaling challenges

Adding new capabilities requires integrating multiple tools and managing complex dependencies. Growth is limited by the capacity to coordinate and maintain an expanding ecosystem of isolated agents.

Fig. 5 Complex system architecture
